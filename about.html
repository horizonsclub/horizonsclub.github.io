Horizons: The Punahou Student Journal

About

History

Horizons: The Punahou Student Journal was founded in early 2025 by a group of students who recognized a gap in how science was communicated and understood within the school community. Inspired by their own experiences navigating scientific research and writing, they envisioned a platform where students could explore complex topics, improve their communication skills, and share their insights with peers.

What began as a few conversations between classmates quickly grew into a fully structured initiative, complete with an editorial board, peer review, and a public-facing website. Built entirely by students‚Äîfrom the articles to the codebase‚ÄîHorizons represents a collaborative effort to make science not only accessible, but student-driven.

Our Mission

The purpose of Horizons: The Punahou Student Journal is to foster intellectual curiosity, critical thinking, and scientific communication, by providing a platform for students to publish high-quality topic summaries and case studies across the diverse fields of the biomedical sciences.

Our Goals

The following are the goals that Horizons sets out to accomplish:

1. Encourage students to explore scientific topics beyond the classroom.

2. Develop members' writing, editing, and communication skills through publication.

3. Foster a supportive environment for peer learning, mentorship, and collaboration.

How We Work

All articles published on Horizons undergo a collaborative process that includes idea pitching, peer feedback, editing, and review by our editorial team. We provide training resources and mentorship to support students at every stage of the writing process‚Äîwhether they're first-time writers or returning contributors.
